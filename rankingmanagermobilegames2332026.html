Ranking Every Football Manager Mobile Game from Worst to Best


15 Best Offline Football Manager Games on Mobile (Ranked S–D Tier)

Today, I'll be playing 15 of the best offline football manager games on mobile. I'll also be rating each of the games from S to D tier and sharing my honest thoughts about them.

1. World Soccer Champs (A Tier)

Starting off with World Soccer Champs. It’s considered one of the best under management for its unique gameplay. The game provides dynamic data packs that allow access to real player names, club names, and logos.

You can choose your starting region, but unlike other games, you must begin with lower-rated teams unless you spend bux. The menu is clean and easy to navigate, and the tactical system allows full control—team mentality, pressure style, and more.

You can buy and sell players with negotiations, and even control players directly by swiping shots during matches. You act as both manager and player, which is a cool feature. The game also includes a news hub and loan system. Overall, it’s a peak and enjoyable experience.

2. Foot Lord (C Tier)

Foot Lord is an interesting and underrated game. You can create a custom manager and choose a club to manage. It features multiple leagues and a simple, clean interface.

The game includes transfers, contract negotiations, and tactical setups like formations and play styles. It’s calm and relaxing, perfect for players who don’t want too much complexity.

3. New Star Manager (B Tier)

New Star Manager is a nostalgic favorite. You manage a team while also controlling players during matches through swiping mechanics.

The game includes facility upgrades, staff hiring, and a card system for boosts. While tactics feel limited, the animations and overall gameplay still make it enjoyable.

4. RFM 24 (D Tier)

RFM 24 feels outdated and stuck in the past. While it includes real player names and leagues, the interface is confusing and poorly designed.

Transfers are instant with no negotiations, and tactical options are extremely limited. Overall, it’s not recommended at all.

5. Idle Eleven (A Tier)

Idle Eleven is a fun and engaging game focused on player upgrades and earnings. Players generate income based on their level, and you can build a stronger team over time.

The game includes leagues, boosters, and strategic decision-making. It’s simple but very addictive and enjoyable.

6. Pocket Manager Football (D Tier)

This feels like a direct copy of RFM 24 with no improvements. Nothing new is added, making it an easy D tier.

7. FCM 26 (S Tier)

FCM 26 is one of my favorite games. You take the role of a football chairman, focusing on club ownership and management.

You can build staff, manage finances, upgrade facilities, and interact with the team manager. The UI is user-friendly, and the simulation system with commentary is excellent. No intrusive ads make it even better.

8. Pocket Manager Football (D Tier)

This version had great background music, but that’s where the positives end. The interface is confusing, gameplay is unclear, and matches happen without proper interaction. Overall, it’s disappointing.

9. Club Boss (C Tier)

Club Boss is a simple and relaxing game. You build your team, hire staff, and manage sponsors.

Matches are quick simulations, and the game is beginner-friendly. It’s decent but not outstanding.

10. Football Team Manager (C Tier)

This game looks simple but is actually confusing. It includes advanced tactics and stadium upgrades, but they don’t work effectively.

Match simulations are unclear, and gameplay lacks engagement. Still, it gets a C tier for effort.

11. Football Tycoon 2 (C Tier)

Football Tycoon 2 has a simple UI and allows squad management, fixtures tracking, and facility upgrades.

However, it lacks tactical depth, which limits its overall appeal.

12. Super Kick Off (S Tier)

Super Kick Off is highly underrated. It includes multiple leagues worldwide, including local leagues, with impressive accuracy.

The game is offline-friendly, has licensed teams, and allows transfers, stadium upgrades, and basic tactics. It’s relaxing and doesn’t push in-game purchases.

13. Football Agent (B Tier)

Football Agent offers a unique experience where you act as a player agent. You scout talents, negotiate contracts, and build your agency.

It’s a refreshing take on football management and highly recommended for hardcore fans.

14. Cyber Foot (C Tier)

Cyber Foot is simple and outdated but easy to play. It includes tactical options, substitutions, and match tracking.

While relaxing, it may not appeal to modern gamers.

15. FTM 26 (S Tier)

FTM 26 is the best offline football manager game on mobile. You play as a head coach or manager with full control over tactics and team development.

The game features advanced tactics, real-time simulations with highlights, press interactions, and club development systems. It delivers a complete football management experience.

Final Thoughts

These are some of the best offline football manager games available on mobile right now. From top-tier experiences like FTM 26 and FCM 26 to casual games like Idle Eleven, there’s something for everyone.

If you feel like I missed any great games, drop them in the comments and share your thoughts!
