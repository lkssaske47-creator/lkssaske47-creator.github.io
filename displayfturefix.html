solution for display over other apps feature not available [ FIX ]


The "Display over other apps" feature on Android allows one app to show content on top of other apps, even when it’s not actively in use. This feature is commonly used by apps that provide floating widgets, chat heads, or other interactive elements that appear on top of the current screen.

However, if you're experiencing issues or it's not available on your Android phone, it could be due to a few reasons:

1. Permissions: You may need to enable the permission manually for specific apps. To do so: Go to Settings > Apps > Special access > Display over other apps. Find the app in the list and toggle it on.

2. Android Version: Some older Android versions or certain customized Android skins (like Samsung's One UI, MIUI for Xiaomi phones, etc.) may limit or manage this feature differently.

3. Battery Optimization: Some devices may restrict this feature in battery-saving modes. Turning off battery optimization for the relevant apps might resolve the issue.

4. Device Restrictions: Some manufacturers or carriers may disable this feature in custom ROMs or specific device models.

5. App-Specific Settings: In some cases, apps themselves may not support or allow the feature depending on how they are designed.

If it's unavailable on your device, checking app-specific settings and the Android version could help identify the issue.
